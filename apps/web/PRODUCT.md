# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary users are freelancers and small agencies seeking early liquidity, plus investors evaluating short-duration receivables. Buyers use a focused wallet-free repayment page. Operators and judges inspect agent traces, Casper transactions, webhook verification, and settlement health.

## Product Purpose

Cortex turns unpaid invoices into AI-underwritten, USD-normalized receivables funded and settled on Casper. Success means the complete evidence-to-settlement path is legible, trustworthy, and operational: upload, underwriting, listing, funding, verified Dodo repayment, on-chain settlement, and investor claim.

## Positioning

Cortex makes Casper the verifiable trust layer for agent-managed invoice financing: evidence is auditable, money math is deterministic, and repayment is recognized only after verified web2 payment proof reaches on-chain state.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, decisive, and credible. The interface should feel like a financial control surface with the clarity of a well-run settlement desk—not a speculative crypto product or theatrical AI demo.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI landing-page scaffolds, crypto-casino aesthetics, placeholder stock imagery, decorative glow and grain, glassmorphism, repetitive card grids, fake AI theatre, and visual effects that obscure financial state. Never imply repayment from a redirect, hide critical transaction proof, or make important actions look decorative.

## Design Principles

- Make the financial state unmistakable before adding visual expression.
- Show evidence, hashes, checks, and transaction proof instead of asking for trust.
- Keep web3 invisible until signing or verification makes it relevant.
- Use decisive hierarchy: one primary task per surface, with supporting detail progressively disclosed.
- Preserve the same interaction vocabulary across seller, investor, buyer, agent, and operator workflows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain visible keyboard focus, 44px touch targets for primary controls, readable contrast, semantic status labels that do not rely on color alone, reduced-motion alternatives, and responsive layouts without horizontal overflow.
